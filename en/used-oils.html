Professional approach to
hazardous waste

Used oils are waste generated from the operation of industrial, automotive, and hydraulic systems. Although they appear to be ordinary liquids, their composition poses a serious threat to the environment and human health.

After use, oils lose their lubricating properties and become saturated with: heavy metals (lead, cadmium, chromium); fuel residues and combustion products; carcinogenic substances and toxins.

Challenges of disposal: collecting used oils requires airtight containers and the exclusion of contaminants, necessitating special infrastructure. When used without treatment, oil releases dioxins and heavy metals. Many service stations and private companies dispose of it improperly — dumping it into sewers or burning it, causing harm to the environment.

Our company provides a complete cycle of safe disposal of used oils:
Collection and transportation in accordance with licenses and regulations; Processing using filtration and regeneration.

Disposal of residual fractions at specialised facilities; We collaborate with industrial entities, energy complexes, service stations, agricultural enterprises, and shipping companies, among others.

If your organisation is faced with the need to dispose of used oils — contact us. We know how to do it correctly.

How does it work?

Purification and regeneration
Oils go through several stages: filtration, degassing, removal of water and mechanical impurities. Part of the product is regenerated into base oil or fuel.

Acceptance and analysis
We accept mineral, synthetic, industrial and motor oils - even mixed or impure. We carry out laboratory control.

Safe disposal of residues Everything that cannot be recovered is disposed of in accordance with environmental regulations - without harming the environment.

Why is this important?

Proper recycling of oils prevents soil and water pollution, preserving nature for future generations.

Reusing oil components reduces the need for extracting new raw materials and helps conserve energy resources.

Rational use is the key to sustainable development.

Used oils contain many valuable components that can be regenerated and reused in technical processes. This reduces the need for crude oil extraction—a depleting resource that requires significant costs for extraction and transportation. By using recycled materials, we reduce our carbon footprint, lessen the burden on nature, and promote the development of a circular economy.

Compliance with environmental legislation is not just a requirement, but an advantage.

According to Ukrainian and European legislation, companies are required to properly store, transport, and dispose of hazardous waste, including used oils. Our company provides full support: from document preparation to issuing a disposal certificate.

One drop of used oil can contaminate up to 1,000 liters of water.

If these substances enter the soil or water bodies, they cause irreparable harm to ecosystems. Safe recycling is not a formality, but a real contribution to preserving nature and protecting human health.
