Professional approach to
hazardous waste

Technical residues are by-products generated during the processing of oil, fuel production, chemicals, or other industrial operations.

They are not always suitable for direct use and generally require recycling, disposal, or special storage.

Technical waste is hazardous waste classified as categories I–III of danger.
Violating storage or disposal regulations can lead to soil, water, and air contamination.
Their processing requires a license and adherence to environmental standards.

What happens to the waste:
Collection and transportation in accordance with the law.
Recycling or regeneration at specialized facilities (e.g., pyrolysis, cracking, centrifugation).

Neutralization or stabilization before landfill disposal.
Waste passporting is a mandatory procedure for control and accounting.

If your organization faces the need to dispose of TECHNICAL RESIDUES — contact us. We know how to do it right.

How does it work?

Initial cleaning and extraction of harmful or recoverable components.

Receiving and classifying residues to determine the processing method.

Recycling with the possibility of resource recovery (e.g., oils or metals), or safe disposal of residues.

Why is this important?

Technical residues may contain toxic or flammable substances that harm the environment and health.

Their proper disposal helps avoid leaks, fires, fines, and supports ecological safety for enterprises.

Waste that is brought back to life

From one litre of used oil, up to 70% of valuable base oil can be recovered — a resource that works again for production.

1 litre of oil equals one million litres of contaminated water.

One drop of carelessly disposed oil can cause a disaster — pollution, fire, and poisoning of ecosystems.

Waste as raw material for new production

Recycled technical residues become the basis for new materials in metallurgy, energy, and industry — this is the circular economy in action.
