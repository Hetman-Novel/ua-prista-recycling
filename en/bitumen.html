Professional approach to
hazardous waste

Bituminous waste is a by-product generated during the production and use of petroleum products, particularly in road construction, waterproofing, and roofing works. Despite its widespread use, the disposal of bitumen remains a serious environmental and technological challenge.

Bitumen itself is a complex mixture of hydrocarbons with a high content of resins, sulfur, and heavy metals. When decomposed or burned, it releases toxic compounds, including dioxins and polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs), making improper handling extremely hazardous to human health and the environment.

Our company employs certified methods for handling bituminous waste that comply with European and international environmental standards.

We offer: Collection and transportation of bituminous residues in accordance with regulations; Thermal processing with emission control; Disposal in isolated facilities when recycling is not possible.

Bituminous waste is not just construction debris, but a hazardous material that requires a professional approach. Only specialized enterprises with the appropriate equipment and permits can ensure safe disposal without harming the environment.

If your organization is faced with the need to dispose of bitumen, contact us. We know how to do it right.

How does it work?

Thermal or mechanical processing We apply environmentally safe technologies to separate bitumen from impurities.

Receiving and sorting We determine the type and quality of the bituminous material.

Reusing The obtained fractions are used in:
producing new asphalt concrete;
in waterproofing systems;
in thermal insulation materials.

Why is this important?

The recycling of bitumen helps to reduce construction costs, decrease the consumption of natural resources, and lower CO₂ emissions. It is a path towards sustainable production and savings for businesses.

Bitumen waste pollutes the soil and water if not disposed of properly. Safe disposal is essential for protecting the environment, human health, and complying with environmental regulations.

Environmental protection — reducing harmful impacts

Bituminous materials, especially after road repairs or roof dismantling, contain heavy metals, polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons, and other toxic substances. Without proper treatment, they harm soils, water, and air. We turn this threat into a resource through modern cleaning and reuse methods.

A profitable solution for the construction business

Recycling bitumen saves up to 70% on raw materials, eliminates disposal costs, and meets eco-standards. This is not an expense — it’s an investment in profit and reputation.

Infrastructure of the future: based on renewable materials

Using recycled bitumen reduces waste, lowers construction costs, and speeds up repairs. This is the key to smart urban development.
