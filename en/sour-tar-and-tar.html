Professional approach to
hazardous waste

Acidic tar is a toxic, difficult-to-dispose-of waste that is formed during the purification of petroleum products using concentrated sulfuric acid. It is a viscous, resinous mass with a high content of sulfur, organic acids, and hydrocarbons.

Acidic tar is produced during acid purification: of gasoline, kerosene, oils, and other hydrocarbon fractions. After the reaction of sulfuric acid with unwanted impurities in oil, a precipitate forms — acidic tar, which contains: residues of sulfuric acid (up to 15–20%), polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs), resins, and acids.

Hazard class: I–II (high), depending on the composition.
Environmental threat: leakage into soil or water bodies causes long-term pollution.
Toxicity: contains carcinogenic compounds.

Acidic tar requires a specialized approach:
Thermal treatment; Chemical neutralization;
Pyrolysis — in combination with neutralization of acidic components; Stabilization and disposal.

Illegal or unauthorized storage of acidic tar is punishable by law in many EU and CIS countries.

If your organization faces the need to dispose of Acidic tar or tar — contact us. We know how to do it correctly.

How does it work?

Chemical or thermal neutralisation of acidity and toxic components.

Removal and preliminary stabilisation of tar to prevent leaks and harmful evaporation.

Regeneration of useful substances or complete disposal in accordance with environmental standards.

Why is this important?

The recycling of acidic tar allows us to avoid extremely dangerous impacts on the environment and human health.

At the same time, it opens up the possibility of reusing valuable hydrocarbons, reducing the need for primary extraction.

Tar is an ecological time bomb for soils and water bodies.

Unprocessed acidic tar poisons the land for decades, seeping into groundwater and spreading toxic vapours. Its discharge is a direct blow to the environment, human health, and future generations.

We transform toxic waste into road surfaces.

From tar, we can obtain binding components for asphalt and technical fuel. Instead of dead sludge, we create new roads, energy, and infrastructure. This is an example of true circular economy.

It is more cost-effective than fines and production stoppages.

Violations in tar management can cost millions: from accidents and lawsuits to loss of licenses. Timely recycling is not just a duty, but a strategic advantage for business.
